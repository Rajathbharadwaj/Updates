There is also an alternative training method, the continuous bag of words, that feeds the model the surrounding (context) words and then gives a prediction for the target word. The output vector is a list of probabilities for all the words in the vocabulary of being one of the context words for the target/input word. The order of the words is random, and all words contribute in a direction orthogonal to all other words. To achieve this, we must use the order in which words occur in sentences to create a vector that capture some of the meaning of words. It is easy to come up with examples of words with similar spelling (or even anagrams) where the meaning of the words are definitely not the same. It works by counting up all the words in a document and assigning each word in the chosen vocabulary to a position in a vector representation.
