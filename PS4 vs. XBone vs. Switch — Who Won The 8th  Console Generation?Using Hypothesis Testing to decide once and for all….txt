One could argue that the fairest way to measure which out of two consoles is best is to examine only the games that appear on both. Having said that, the difference seems small (just over 1 point on average), and the range is quite wide either side of the mean. All things considered, can we conclude that the average difference between platforms is significant? In particular, we will lean on a game’s ‘MetaCritic score’ as a measure of its ‘intrinsic quality’. Again, we need to remember that we are dealing with samples, not populations. Welch’s T-Test is a variation on our regular T-Test that deals much better with samples with differing sizes and variances. Then the distribution of that population would, of course, have a mean of 0.
