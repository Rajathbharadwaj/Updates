This method, also known as Beer’s method, can be used to estimate the concentration of different components in an unknown spectrum. However, because the measured spectrum and the pure substances spectra contain noise, there will be, in general, no exact solution for the equation A = Kc. To do so, the unknown spectrum is searched using a search algorithm which computes a score based on a similarity metric, providing a list of nearest matches or concentrations. In this short tutorial, the Classical Least Squares method to calculate the similarity between spectra will be explained.
