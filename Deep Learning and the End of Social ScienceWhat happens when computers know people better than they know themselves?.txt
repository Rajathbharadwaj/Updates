One could, at least in theory, build software that teaches children to read via any chosen method, measures their progress, and evaluates the success of each approach. With only a little more work, one could build the software to learn from children even as it helps them learn to read. To begin, you have to design a study that offers some children phonics teaching, and other children whole language, in a way that is fair to both methods. Deep learning algorithms, unlike scientific ones, care not a bit about understanding. More likely, however, it would converge on new strategies for teaching children to read that don’t chime with either of the warring theories. Ever-improving methods for erecting models of how the world works and then testing those models against evidence make it possible to distinguish good ideas from bad. Even if you could do all those things, would it be ethical to persist if it started to look like one method was more effective than the other?
