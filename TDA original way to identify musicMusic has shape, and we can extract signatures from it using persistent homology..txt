In music theory, Tonnetz is a lattice diagram that represents pitch space, allowing to capture certain harmonic relationships in musical data (see figures below). Persistent homology is thus claimed to be stable under small changes in the input filtration (so that such changes lead to small perturbations in the bottleneck distance). In this article, I introduce my approach to music identification, applying ideas from the field of Topological Data Analysis (TDA). We could also use discrete Gaussian curvature as our height function to reflect different geometries of the Tonnetz. In short, the idea is to build filtrations of data and see how certain homology group invariants (Betti numbers) change at different spatial resolutions. As input to the algorithm, I use .WAV files containing 1, 2, 3, 4, 5-seconds clips of songs.
