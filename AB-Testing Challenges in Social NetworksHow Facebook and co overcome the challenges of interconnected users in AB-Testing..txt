If user A is in the treatment group and is connected with user B in the control group, user A’s change in behaviour might impact user B’s behavior. Influencers, who play a big role in these networks, are a relatively small group of users that are able to have a great impact on many users. On the other side, the higher the number of disparate clusters within the user group, the less isolated those clusters will be. This is since users in the control group might be encouraged to engage more with their feed as well. But also in less extreme cases spillover effects might not be reduced by simply clustering users in groups with their closest connections. Ultimately those results can be used to calculate the average treatment effect.This method can also be used to prove the existence of network effects in general. Metrics such as conversion rate are first calculated on a cluster level, and then again averaged on a treatment group level.
