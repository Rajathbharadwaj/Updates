Pay attention to q(f):We cannot use the multivariate Gaussian marginalization rule to simply read off the marginal distribution q(f) from the joint distribution q(f, fₛ). Variational inference uses a new distribution q(f, fₛ), called the variational distribution, to approximate the true posterior p(f, fₛ|y). So p(y) is the average likelihood of the data y, with all possible values of f and fₛ accounted for, through the weights p(f, fₛ). That’s the meaning of q(f, fₛ) approximating the posterior p(f, fₛ|y).The variational inference technique gives us the ELBO formula. Every different value of f and fₛ generates a different data probability via the likelihood p(y|f, fₛ).
