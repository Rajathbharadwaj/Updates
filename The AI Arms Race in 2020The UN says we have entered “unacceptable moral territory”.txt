Almost three in every four Europeans would like an international treaty prohibiting lethal autonomous weapons, according to a poll conducted in November by Human Rights Watch. How should we compare China’s development of autonomous aerial drones and hypersonic ‘smart’ missiles with America’s recent $2.7bn unveiling of huge unmanned ships? Military operations have had to be completely re-thought — physical distancing on a submarine is much harder than physical distancing in a supermarket. It will be no surprise that these countries — Russia, China and the United States — have by far the most advanced military AI. What about Russia’s autonomous tanks, deployed for the first time into combat in Syria? Lethal AI already had some mounting advantages over human equivalents, and can now add ‘immunity from catastrophic viruses’ to that list. TIME has detailed how Russia has “steamrolled” the process by holding up discussion documents and postponing meetings.
