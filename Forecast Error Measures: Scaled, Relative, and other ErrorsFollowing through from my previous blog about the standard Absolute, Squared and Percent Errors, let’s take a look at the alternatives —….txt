They depend on another reference forecast to evaluate itself, and more often than not, in practice, the reference forecast is a Naïve Forecast or a Seasonal Naïve Forecast. But Scaled Error is different here because it is not directly dependent on the Reference Forecast, but rather on the mean absolute error of the reference forecast. This measure can be anything really, and not just MAE.Relative Error is based on a reference forecast, although most commonly we use Naïve Forecast, not necessarily all the time. If we keep the Reference Forecast constant and vary the error, the measures are symmetric on both sides of the errors. If the Ground Truth is too close to Reference Forecast(in this case the Naïve Forecast), the errors are going to be much higher. And because of the low reliability of RMSE and the favoring low forecast issue of MAPE, they suggest to use GMRAE(Geometric Mean Relative Absolute Error). To compare this they examined the rankings of the forecast methods as before, but this time they compared the rankings between pairs of error measures.
