It might be the case that, in a certain point in the future, less overall human work is needed, and people become less relevant. When we really start mixing biological tissue and robotics, will these robots be considered people, or something else in between? Many questions have come up because of those features, such as: is it ok to make a robot that looks a lot like a celebrity (or someone you know)? Will we start creating unrealistic expectations towards other people, based on our experiences with robots? What happens then?Many people bet on Universal Basic Income: the idea that the government would give a minimum level of income to everyone. This type of reasoning can literally save lives down the road.The recent technological advances require new legislation, to answer the moral questions that have never been asked before. That is fine, it allows us to work on more significant endeavors, while robots get the boring ones.
