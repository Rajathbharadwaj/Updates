We calculate a baseline score via always predicting the most common city and achieve a score of 0.1 F1. Symmetry means that the correlation is the same whether you calculate the correlation of A and B or the correlation of B and A. The score should be 0 if there is no relationship and the score should be 1 if there is a perfect relationship. As we can see, a PPS score of 0.94 is rather high, so the zip code seems to have a good predictive power towards the city. Of course, the score should be asymmetric because I want to detect all the weird relationships between cities and zip codes. The score ranges from -1 to 1 and indicates if there is a strong linear relationship — either in a positive or negative direction. In fact, there are many possible ways to calculate a score that satisfies the requirements mentioned before.
