Meet the data charlatan!Attracted by the lure of lucrative jobs, these hucksters give legitimate data professionals a bad name. Unlike charlatans, they don’t make conclusions beyond their data.Analysts are exempt from calling their shots… as long as they aren’t reaching beyond their data. A core principle of data science is that if you’re dealing with uncertainty, it’s not valid to use the same datapoint for both hypothesis generation and for testing. The charlatan is sinister character who shows you how well their model performed… on the same data they used to make the model. When you have limited data, uncertainty forces you to choose between statistics or analytics. It’s called data-splitting and in my opinion it’s the most powerful idea in data science.Never take an untested opinion seriously.
