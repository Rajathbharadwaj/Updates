Each word is embedded in isolation, and every word contains a single one and N zeros where N is the number of dimensions. From here, our training sample of input-output pairs would be (“ate”, “I”), (“ate”, “an”), and (“ate”, “apple”). Again, skip-gram is about predicting context words appearing near the input word. Instead of doing that, the skip-gram neural network model selects a smaller number of features, or neurons, that say something more useful about that word.
