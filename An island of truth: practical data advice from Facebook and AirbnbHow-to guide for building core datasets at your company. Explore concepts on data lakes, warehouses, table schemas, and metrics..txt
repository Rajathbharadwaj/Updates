You were dedicated and thoughtful in building Core Data, helping Airbnb become a world-class company for people to work with data. At first glance, the Airbnb data lake already looked pretty daunting, with thousands of tables and nearly a petabyte of data. My suggestion is to create a small set of data engineering experts who can help find a balance between rigid processes and supporting the faster pace of product development. Core Data at its heart was a set of fact tables and dimension tables based on practical subject areas. We lacked a credible, single source of truth for important data tables and key metrics. The benefit was more people had access to Hive and therefore could easily join these trusted Core Data tables to their own datasets. People new to Airbnb could confidently begin their data journeys knowing that these tables were trustworthy.
