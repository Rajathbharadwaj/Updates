In order to help alleviate this, it is a good idea to have people within an engineering team rotate around a number of areas a team is responsible for. Where PRs help keep the detailed low-level knowledge flowing, collaboration, feedback and review of designs help the higher-level knowledge spread. A low Lottery Factor therefore is bad, because people do leave projects for many reasons, and having a project dependent on one or two key people is risky. But, in order to maintain a good Lottery Factor, that knowledge should also be duplicated in fragments across a number of other people. This could be due to attrition or just the team focusing on the newer aspects of a project or new projects entirely. In this situation, a decision would need to be made as to whether a single replacement SME collating all the knowledge to become the replacement is the desired outcome. Accepting and facilitating feedback on designs and plans also encourages buy-in from others on the project, which promotes shared ownership and responsibilities.
