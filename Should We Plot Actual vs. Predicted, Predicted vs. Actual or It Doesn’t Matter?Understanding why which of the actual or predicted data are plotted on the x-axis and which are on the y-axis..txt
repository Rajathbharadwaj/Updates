Plotting the actual and predicted data is frequently used for visualizing and analyzing how the actual data correlate with those predicted by the model. On the other hand, when plotted as predicted vs. actual, the intercept increases with an increase of the noise level, while the slope decreases. However, R-squared has a similar behavior regardless of which axis the predicted data are plotted. However, it can sometimes be confusing to exactly know which data should be plotted on the x-axis and which one should be on the y-axis. In both cases, the distribution of the slope and intercept differs significantly as the noise increases. Indeed, by a quick search on Google, one finds many results with predicted on the x-axis or on the y-axis. Ideally, this should correspond to a slope of 1 and an intercept of 0.
