One-to-one matching (M=1) would match the “safe” koala with the “fiery” koala who eats 220 kg (as this is the closest to 200 kg). In practice, we decide on a number (M) of “safe” koalas we want to use as a composite match for our “fiery” koala. Let’s say we want to match for a koala who eats 200 kg per year and are considering two potential koalas who eat 179 and 220 kg. To do so, it pairs up data points from the treatment group with data points from the control group — based on their similarity on chosen characteristics. Subtracting the mean of the outcome variable of the control group from the same mean of the treatment group will therefore hint at the potential treatment effect. Multiple matching involves pairing the treated unit with multiple control units.
