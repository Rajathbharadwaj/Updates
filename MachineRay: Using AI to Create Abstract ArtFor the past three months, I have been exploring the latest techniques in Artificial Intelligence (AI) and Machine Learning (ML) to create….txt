I used a technique called Image Augmentation to increase the number of images by a factor of 7, making it 11,900 images. Note that I filtered the images to only get paintings that were labeled in the “Abstract” genre, and only images that are labeled as being in the Public Domain. In order to have a variety of aspect ratios for the output images, I imposed a new aspect ratio prior to the upscaling. These include images that were published before 1925 or images that were created by artists who died before 1950. Here are some pictures of source paintings before and after the frame removal.Although 850 images may seem like a lot, it’s not really enough to properly train a GAN. To make the source images consistent, and to allow the GAN to focus on the content of the paintings, I automatically removed the frames using a Python script.
