Graph neural networks can be seen as attempting to find a graph embedding f(G), with the desired property f(G)=f(G′) iff G~G′. While important insights into the expressive power of graph neural networks have emerged from the link to the graph isomorphism problem, the setting itself is rather limited. Second, the graph isomorphism problem does not provide any guarantees for approximately isomorphic graphs, in which case d(G,G′)>0. Last but not least, the question of graph neural network expressivity has been primarily focused on the topology of the graphs. In the context of deep learning on graphs, it was shown that message passing neural networks are as powerful as the 1-WL test .
