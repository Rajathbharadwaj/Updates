Despite the relatively low number of people suffering per disease, the total number of people affected by one rare disease or another is quite high. But the deep learning frenzy among academic researchers who previously had zero interest in machine learning underlies an important reality. The goal of this essay is to discuss meaningful machine learning progress in the real-world application of drug discovery. Many of the results are likely over-fitting their small datasets and not many make substantial contributions to either the basic science under study or machine learning. However, with deep learning for drug discovery we are now able to predict so much more! A side effect of the flurry of enthusiasm surrounding deep learning for biomedical image analysis is the unavoidable increase in noise. Based on automated high-throughput microscopy and machine learning, they produce vast expanses of new data with robotic microscopes and liquid handling.
