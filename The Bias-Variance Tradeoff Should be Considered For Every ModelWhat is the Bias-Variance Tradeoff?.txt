The more inputs the more a model may understand about your training data, but potentially the less it will be capable of generalizing to data it has never seen. In fact, that model may perform very well on its training data. A common phrase you might hear is that a high bias model is ‘over generalized’. Nothing new here; what is new is the idea that said error is actually made up of two things… You guessed it, bias & variance! Questions about the bias-variance tradeoff are used very frequently in interviews for data scientist positions. I’d boil it all down to consideration. Makes sense!
