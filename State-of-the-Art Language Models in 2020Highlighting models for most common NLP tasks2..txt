For token level tasks such as sequence tagging or question answering, hidden states of each token are fed to an output layer. On the other hand, for encoder-decoder attention, queries are extracted from the previous decoder layer, whereas keys and values are from encoder hidden states. Each input sequence starts with a special [CLS] token, the hidden state of which is used as a representation for classification tasks. Furthermore, the paper proposed Multi-Head Attention, such that the model can attend to information from different representation subspaces. Computationally, the query stream is trainable, and the content stream is initialized with corresponding word embeddings.A large number of permutations cause slow training convergence of the model. Thus, closing the gap between language modeling and actual downstream tasks which often requires bidirectional information. However, an encoder-decoder attention layer is inserted between self-attention and feed-forward layer.
