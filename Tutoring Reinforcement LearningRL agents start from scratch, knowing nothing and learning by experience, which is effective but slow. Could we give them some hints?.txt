As you can see, the DQN agent with Tutor4RL shows an initial good performance thanks to the tutor guide while the plain DQN agent struggles. The tutor will reply with an ensemble of its functions that the agent will then use to choose what action to execute and learn from this experience. There are two types of knowledge functions:The agent can ask the tutor for its knowledge when it’s uncertain about what to do, for example, in its initial steps. It takes about 1.3 million iterations for the plain DQN agent to catch up and in iteration 1.5 million the tutor ceases to be used completely. When τ is greater than a sample taken from U(0,1) distribution, we use the tutor’s output, otherwise, we use the policy’s output. Until then, the agent’s performance will be far from great.
