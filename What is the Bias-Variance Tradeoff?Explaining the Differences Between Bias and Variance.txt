A model that is too complex is a model that is overfitting and a model that is too simple is underfitting. When it comes to Data Science, there comes a point where one has to make sure that their Machine Learning model is “just right”. In the case of underfitting, the model is unable to fit to the training data and as a result, will be unable to generalize new data. Overfitting will result in a model that models the training data too well; detecting patterns and noise in the data that are not actually there. This will give us a model that is not too simple (underfitting of the data) and not too complex (overfitting of the data). As data scientists, we must search for a model that strikes the perfect or at least close to perfect balance between the two. Bias can come in many different forms such as Sampling Bias or Selection Bias.Variance is also an error in the machine learning model.
