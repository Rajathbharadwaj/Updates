Model C includes significant negative coefficients for mean words per line and mean word length, meaning that the larger these terms are, the lower the episode’s rating, on average. The sparklines show the median number of characters appearing in each episode, by show and season. Compared to this, the text summary terms, together with show and season (as numeric), do show a small amount of predictive skill. Noun-to-verb and noun-to-adjective ratios, fractions of lines spoken by each of the main characters, and eventually term frequencies (100 common words and bigrams), were also included as features. Noun-adjective ratio was not found to be predictive of episode rating.Character line fractions provide further lift, when added to the show terms (model D). The clustering seen here is probably a reflection of the biases of the writers, who are writing for all of the characters in the show at once. As can also be seen in the grid above, Marcy and Peggy show a surprising similarity with the characters of Frasier.
